Frequently Asked Questions

What is pressure washing and how does it work?

Pressure washing or pressure washing is the use of high-pressure water spray to remove loose paint, mold, grime, dust, mud and dirt from surfaces and objects such as buildings, furniture and concrete surfaces.

Is it better to pressure wash a house or wash it with soft washing?

Some materials are easily damaged by high-pressure water. Soft washing, on the other hand, effectively cleans contaminants like dirt, mold, and mildew from these surfaces. For this reason, it is better to use the gentle washing method on more delicate exterior coverings.

Do pressure washing companies use customers' water?

It depends on the area and type of project. Most pressure washing companies connect to the faucet to get the water directly. Since the water is most often on site, the company will not need to bring water to your home, which simplifies the process and reduces costs. In some other cases, special units with independent water tanks will be used.

Can you pressure wash your house yourself?

Most pressure washers can expel water at 3000 PSI. It's powerful enough to punch holes in some siding materials and glass windows, destroy wood deck surfaces, or erode cement from your driveway or parking lot. If not handled correctly, these machines can cause serious injuries.
